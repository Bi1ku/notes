Baker v. Carr

Facts:
  - By this time, Tenesse hasn't redrawn districts in over 60 yrs.
  - Urban populations had grown much faster, but since districts weren't redrawn, rural voters had more power than urban
  - Arguemnt was that it violated the 14th amendment because not everyone was equal under the law.

Decisions:
  - Issues of reapportionment and redistricting were within their jurisdiction
  - Ruled that they were right. Votes were not equal

Why it matters:
  - Established one person, one vote doctrine
    - States had to redistrict their land in a way so that all are equally represented fairly
  - Fundamentally altered natura of political representation
  - Expanded court's jurisdiction
