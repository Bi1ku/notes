Brown v. Board of Education

Facts:
  - Segregated schools in sourth bc of plessy v. ferguson
  - black family tried to enroll daughter in white school that was closer
    - denied, required to go to black school farther away
  - brown argued that violated equal protection clause of 14th amendment
    - Seperating people was inherently unequal

Decision:
  - Separate facilities violated equal protection clause
  - "seperate but equal" has no place

Why it matters?:
  - Massive victory for civil rights movement
  - Overturned Plessy v. Ferguson
  - Desegregation
