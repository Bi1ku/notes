Citizens United v. FEC

Facts:
  - Campaign finace laws (esp. Bipartisant campaign finance act (bcra))
    - BCRA made it illegal for corporations or non profits to engage in
    electioneering communications for 60 days before an election or
    30 days before a primary
  - Citizens United wanted to release a damning documetary to sway votes
  away from Hilary, but couldn't due to limits
  - Citizens United argued that it violated first amendment speech

Decision:
  - Court agreed with Citizens United (5-4)
  - Basically same as government censorship of speech to individuals
  - Laws that restricted finance and when to release were all unconstitutional
  - Law was struct down

Why does it matter?:
  - Essentially unlimited money for funding
