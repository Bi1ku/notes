McCulloh v. Maryland

Facts:
  - Congress chartered second bank of united states
  - Chartered location in baltimore maryland
    - Maryland didn't like it, so they passed law that taxed it heavily. 15,000 a year tax on the national bank.
      - National bank refused to pay it => brought to court.

Maryland argued that national bank was unconstitutional because Constitution didn't say anything about creating national bank

Mculloch argued that it was neccessary and proper (elastic clause) (implied powers).

Unanimous in favor of McCulloch.

If the law in question upholds the spririt of the consittuion, then it shall stand.

Gave federal government more power.
