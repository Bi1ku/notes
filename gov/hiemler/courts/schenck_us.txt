Schenck v. The United States

Facts:
  - Congress passed the Espionage Act
    - Outlaw hinderances to military recruitments
    - Draft just started that year
  - Schenck didn't like draft, wrote things that encouraged people to avoid it
  - Schenck argued that 1st amendment was violated and that section of espionage
  act was unconstituional

Decesions:
  - Court ruled that SChenck's rights weren't violated because he clearly
  encouraged men to avoid the draft, which is illegal activity.

Why it matters:
  - Clear and present danger test precedent
    - But now replaced by brandenburg test
