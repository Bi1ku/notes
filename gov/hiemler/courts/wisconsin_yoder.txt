Wisconsin v. Yoder

Facts:
  - 3 amish families removed kids after 8th grade
    - Religius beliefs & vocational trainings.
  - Wisconsin had a law that made it required for children at least 16 years of age
  - Yoder argued it violated first amendment right
  - Wisconsin argued that it had a compelling interest to educate the state
  and trumped Amish's ability to free exercise.

Decision:
  - Court ruled in favor of Yoder

Why it matters:
  - Set tone for future cases
  - Homeschool movement
  - Gave more individual liberties to parents
