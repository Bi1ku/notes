Roles and Powers of the President 4/15

Policy Agenda: Policy that the president wants to implement. Informal promises
to voters to gain votes. Using formal and informal powers.

Formal Powers (explicit powers of the president):
  - Ability to veto
  - Pocket Veto (just don't sign it)
  - Commander-in-chief of the armed forces

Informal Powers:
  - Bargaining and persuation. (HEAVY MEDIA SPOTLIGHT).
  - Executive order
    - Order from president that has the force of federal law, but is not actually
    the law
  - Signing statements
  - Executive agreements between another head of state

As long as the president plays by the rules that congress makes, and that the judge
interprets as good, it's good. Also temporary for 4 years.
